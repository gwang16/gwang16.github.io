Dr. Boom

Probability of an unlikely scenario

Posted on January 24, 2017

As I was watching Amaz's stream on Twitch recently, several extremely rare events happened. I began to wonder what would be the most unlikely scenario that I could imagine, and exactly how unlikely it would be. So here it is.

Scenario

Imagine the following scenario. You're going first as mage versus warlock. It's turn 7, you flamestrike to reset the board. You feel pretty smug, with an arcane missles left in your hand and are up 10 HP to your opponent's 4. Missles plus ping would be lethal if your opponent doesn't draw into something good. Your opponent draws, emotes "Greetings", and slams down Dr. Boom, a 7/7 creature that summons two 1/1s, each of which also has deathrattles that deal 1-4 random damage to a random enemy character. End turn.

Neverlucky. You right click his face and mute him. Turn 8. Draw. Your very own Dr. Boom! You stare at your hand and face a decision: do you play arcane missles plus ping and hope that all missles hit face, or play Dr. Boom and missles and hope that the missles hit your opponents bomb which then hits your bomb which then hits his face? Instinctively, the expected damage from the bombs should be higher than the guaranteed 1 damage from the ping. But is this correct? What are the actual probabilities?

Question

The probability of winning this turn with missles + ping is 1.56% (=1/4^3), which is the event that all three missles hit face. Then, what is the probability of dealing 4 damage to the oppponent's face when both of your boards consist of Dr. Boom, and you play arcane missles?

My Solution

There may exist a closed form solution to this problem, but we employ a Monte Carlo simulation method for its relative simplicity and accuracy.

My proposed solution for the probability of winning by playing Dr. Boom and Arcane Missles is 10.3%. This is substantially higher than attempting to win with Arcane Missles + ping (1.56%).
