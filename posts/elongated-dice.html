Elongated Dice

What's the probability of landing on a 1 or a 6?

Posted on January 15, 2017

I came across a very interesting problem in a blog post by Datascope Analytics. Given a fair six sided die, the probability of rolling a 1 or a 6 is pretty straight forward (1/3). Now we change the dice a little. First, note that the opposite ends of a die always adds up to 7, so that if it lands with 6 facing up, then 1 must be on the bottom. Imagine a die rested in this position, but its height changes in such a way that the area of the 1 and 6 faces stay the same, but that of the remaining 4 faces of 2, 3, 4 and 5 increases or decreases depending on how the height of the dice changes. Now, what is the probability of landing on a 1 or 6 for this new dice?

We know that if the height increases, then p(1 or 6) should decrease, because the relative surface area of the remaining 4 faces increases. Vice versa, if the height decreases, we expect p(1 or 6) to decrease. The question is, can we come up with a closed form solution to represent p(1 or 6)?
