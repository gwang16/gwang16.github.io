Riot API Challenge II

Analyzing the impact of the AP item patch

Posted on January 15, 2016

League of Legends (“LoL”, or “League”) is a Multiplayer Online Battle Arena (“MOBA”) Real-time Strategy (“RTS”) video game published by Riot Games (“Riot”). In July 2015, Riot released a major in-game update (Patch 5.13) that included numerous changes to existing AP items, with the goal of increasing item diversity.

Prior to the patch, items such as Deathcap, Luden’s Echo, and Zhonyas were amongst the most popular items purely because of their superior damage statistics. Riot intended to offer “new choices for a broad swathe of players and champions alike”. Patch 5.13 included changes to the cost and statistics of 14 AP items so that they offer real competition for purchase, by allowing players to diversify their item choices depending on the situation.

The purpose of this analysis is to attempt to answer the question of whether Patch 5.13 was successful in bringing about item diversity. The entire project was created in R, using ‘jsonlite’ package for API request, basic R packages for statistical analysis, ‘ggplot2’ for visualization, ‘shiny’ for interactive graphics, ‘rmarkdown’ for documentation, and ‘shinyapps’ for deployment.
